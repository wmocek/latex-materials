\section{Tables}

\subsection{Basic Table}

Table~\ref{tab:basic} shows a simple example of a table in LaTeX.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Basic table example}
\label{tab:basic}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
Item & Quantity & Price \\
\midrule
Apples & 5 & \$2.50 \\
Oranges & 3 & \$1.80 \\
Bananas & 7 & \$3.15 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Advanced Table}

Table~\ref{tab:advanced} shows a more complex table with multiple columns.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Comparison of programming languages}
\label{tab:advanced}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
Language & Year & Type & Performance \\
\midrule
C & 1972 & Compiled & High \\
Python & 1991 & Interpreted & Medium \\
Java & 1995 & Compiled & High \\
JavaScript & 1995 & Interpreted & Medium \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

To include a figure, use:
\begin{verbatim}
\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{filename}
\caption{Your caption here}
\label{fig:yourlabel}
\end{figure}
\end{verbatim}

\subsection{Referencing}

You can reference tables and figures using their labels. For example:
\begin{itemize}
    \item See Table~\ref{tab:basic} for basic data
    \item Refer to Table~\ref{tab:advanced} for language comparison
\end{itemize}
